Possible Strategy

To determine the team with the highest win probability, focus on "Four Factors" advanced statistics over raw scoring:

  • Effective Field Goal Percentage (eFG%) for shooting efficiency
  • Turnover Rate (TOV%) for ball security
  • Offensive Rebound Percentage (ORB%) for extra possessions
  • Free Throw Rate (FTR) for scoring without the clock running

Here is how to analyze these metrics:

1. The Four Factors (Weighted Importance)
  • Effective Field Goal % (eFG% - 40% weight): This adjusts for the fact that 3-pointers are worth more than 2-pointers. A higher eFG% indicates a more efficient offense.
  • Turnover Percentage (TOV% - 25% weight): Measures how often a team turns the ball over per 100 possessions. Lower is better.
  • Offensive Rebounding Percentage (ORB% - 20% weight): Represents the percentage of available offensive rebounds a team grabs. Higher creates more shot opportunities.
  • Free Throw Rate (FTR - 15% weight): Measures how often a team gets to the foul line. High FTR suggests an aggressive offense that draws fouls.
2. Key Efficiency and Team Metrics
  • Assist-to-Turnover Ratio: A 2:1 ratio (20 assists to 10 turnovers) is a strong indicator of elite ball movement and disciplined play.
  • Rebounding Margin: A positive margin indicates a team controls the boards, limiting opponent second chances while gaining their own.
  • Points Allowed per Game: A low score here demonstrates strong defensive performance.
3. Contextual Factors
  • Home/Away Record: A team's performance often changes significantly based on the venue.
  • Recent Trends: Analyze the last 5–10 games to gauge momentum, rather than just the season average.

By comparing these metrics between two teams, you can identify which team is more efficient at shooting, better at taking care of the ball, and more dominant on the boards, which generally translates to a higher chance of winning.
